We check each claim against decades of real history, with the same fixed test for every one, and show what actually happened — including when it failed. We never tell you what to buy. Free, forever, selling nothing.

Why trust it? Every claim links to its evidence, and every test uses the same public scoring rules — win or lose, we show the result.

Our findings

Here's what our own tests appear to show so far — our findings, not certainty, and always open to being wrong:

  • Price moves do tend to keep moving for a while — that part looks real.
  • Nobody actually knows why — and any test that pretends to know why is overselling itself.
  • More data isn't the same as more evidence. A century of daily prices can still be surprisingly few real, independent events.
  • A "pass" only means something next to a control — a nonsense rule that should fail, tested the same way.
  • Famous strategies we hear about today already survived a filter — the ones that failed quietly aren't the ones getting sold to you.

The mirror — our self-check ▾ tap to open
What "the mirror" means (it ends every room): after testing everyone else's claims, we turn the same test on ourselves — in two words: self-check. Three questions, every time: what did this page actually prove, what can it NOT know, and did it ever slip from showing you what IS into telling you what to DO? A page anywhere else that never holds a mirror to itself is telling you something too.
The mirror, on the front door: free in both directions, forever. It shows what's true, teaches you to check it — including on us — and leaves every decision where it belongs. If this site ever asks for money, keys, or belief, it isn't this site anymore.
How to spot fake builds of Xplainsight

This app is public and forkable on purpose — anyone can copy the code, which also means anyone could copy the look and change the guts. Here's the one test that always works, no matter how convincing the imitation:

Xplainsight never connects to your wallet, never asks for a key or seed phrase, and never touches money. Pasting a public address to look something up is the only input that exists anywhere in this app. That's it.

Anything calling itself Xplainsight that asks for more than that — a connect-wallet button, a seed phrase, a "verify your wallet" prompt, a deposit, a login — is not this app, whatever it looks like. The real version literally cannot take your money; that's not a promise, it's how it's built. Report it to us: Xplainsight@duck.com.
