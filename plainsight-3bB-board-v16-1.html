The rule that spots every fake: Xplainsight never connects to your wallet, never asks for a key or seed phrase, and never touches money. Pasting an address is the only input that exists. Any version of this tool asking for more is a counterfeit — no matter how identical it looks. How do I check this page isn't lying?
Try it on yourself. Paste your own address in the box above and see what everyone else can already see about you — including your real scorecard: your win rate next to your actual profit. They're different numbers, and the gap is the most useful thing this tool shows. Is my wallet data private?
A peek through a keyhole, not the whole market. You only see the wallets loaded here. What it shows is real for those wallets — it says nothing about everyone else. This is for learning how betting, winning, and getting wiped out really work — not for copying anyone's trades. Remember: winning and losing — and everything in between — are only real once a bet is closed (cashed out). Until then it's just a number on the screen that can still change.

🪞 The mirror — our self-check ▾ tap to open
What "the mirror" means (it ends every room): after testing everyone else's claims, we turn the same test on ourselves — in two words: self-check. Three questions, every time: what did this page actually prove, what can it NOT know, and did it ever slip from showing you what IS into telling you what to DO? A page anywhere else that never holds a mirror to itself is telling you something too.
The mirror, for a live data board: every number here is real and pulled live — but a board of numbers can still mislead if it's read as advice. It shows what large wallets are doing right now; it does not show why, and it does not say what you should do next. Big positions can be hedges, mistakes, or unrelated to price at all. Read it as a window, not a signal.
