Graduation — the final exam is auditing us

Every page on this project taught you a method for checking someone else's claims. The final exam is turning all of it on this project itself — because a tool that teaches skepticism and exempts itself is selling something. Complete the audit below (your checks live only on this page and vanish when you close it — like everything here). Pass condition: you find at least one real weakness. There is always at least one.

Why no paid course can assign this exam: their business depends on you continuing to need them, so the course can never end with "now verify that we were honest, then leave." Ours can, because free-in-both-directions means our project succeeds exactly when you stop needing it. This page is the off-ramp, built on purpose.

1. Run the survivorship filter on us. Our bookshelf sorts authors by "did a scorekeeper check this?" Ask it of Xplainsight: who independently checked OUR claims? (Honest answer: mostly nobody yet — one builder, one AI, and your audits. That's a real weakness; the open-source vow exists because of it. If you just thought "wait, the AI helping build the skeptic's tool could itself be confidently wrong" — correct, it was, at least six documented times, caught by the builder — a corrected number is itself proof this list stays honest, not proof the corrections stopped. Now you're doing the exam.)
2. Grade one of our claims with the needle. Pick any confident sentence on any of our pages and grade it yourself: FACT-BACKED → UNSUPPORTED. Check whether we cited a source, whether the source says what we say it says, and whether we showed the counter-evidence. Where we wrote "roughly" or "about," check whether the vagueness hides anything.
3. Follow OUR money. The bookshelf's test: where does the seller's money come from — using the method or selling it? Apply it here: this tool is free, sells nothing, takes no keys, holds no coins. But ALSO ask the harder version: what does the builder get? (Honest answer: yes — genuine personal satisfaction, building something real and getting to be right about it. That's a real incentive, not a neutral one. No incentive is zero, including this one.) Decide for yourself whether that incentive could bend the content, and where you'd look for the bend.
4. Check the pipes yourself. Open your browser's network tab on the wallet board and verify with your own eyes: every request goes to the public APIs we named and nowhere else; nothing is sent when you close it. Trust was never the ask — checking was.
5. Find the keyhole's edge. Every gauge here reads a slice: ~101 wallets from one list, a weekly lagged government report, one exchange's data. Name one conclusion the tool could tempt you toward that its slice cannot actually support. (There are several. The sample warning boxes name some — find one they missed.)
6. Catch the framing. We argue both sides — but WE chose which side got the last word on each page, and order is influence. Find one page where you'd have ordered the arguments differently, and notice that noticing this is the most advanced skill on the site.
That's the diploma. Not a certificate — a habit: you just audited the auditor, followed the money of the people warning you about money, and looked for the edge of a tool that told you where its edges were. Nobody can sell you a secret again without you noticing the shape of the pitch. This project succeeded the moment you stopped needing it — which was the design. Take the habit; leave the tool; come back only if it's useful.
The mirror — our self-check ▾ tap to open
What "the mirror" means (it ends every room): after testing everyone else's claims, we turn the same test on ourselves — in two words: self-check. Three questions, every time: what did this page actually prove, what can it NOT know, and did it ever slip from showing you what IS into telling you what to DO? A page anywhere else that never holds a mirror to itself is telling you something too.
The mirror, one last time: if this page felt like it was performing humility to win your trust — good catch, grade that too. The exam has no answer key, because the skill is living without one.
