The needle widget — a nutrition label for market claims

Food gets a nutrition panel; market claims get nothing. This is the missing label: a tiny badge any writer can attach to any claim, grading its evidence on the needle's five-step scale. It's not a hosted service — it's ~30 lines you copy and own. No server, no tracking, nothing to trust: read what you paste.

Live preview — tap each badge:

The S&P 500 fell about 18% in 2022.

High CAPE means poor returns are likely over the next decade.

This coin will 100x by December.

The snippet — paste anywhere in a page, then mark claims with <span class="ps-needle" data-grade="0–4" data-why="..."></span>. Grades: 0 UNSUPPORTED · 1 STRETCHED · 2 UNVERIFIED · 3 SUPPORTED · 4 FACT-BACKED. The label names its own author (the page you're reading), states its scale on tap, and works with zero network access:

v0 honesty: the page's author assigns each grade — this is a labeling standard, not a lie detector. Its protections are transparency (the scale and authorship are stated on every tap) and forkability (the grader's code is the 30 lines above — nobody, including us, controls it after you copy it). The dream endpoint — readers expecting a receipts-label on market claims the way they expect one on food — starts with the label existing. It now does.
