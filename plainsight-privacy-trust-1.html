Privacy & trust, in plain words

No legal fog. Here is exactly what this app does and doesn't do with you.

What we never do

No accounts. There is nothing to sign up for. We couldn't email you if we wanted to.

No wallet connections, ever. The app never asks to connect a wallet, never asks for keys or seed phrases, and never touches money. Anything that asks you for those things is not us.

No selling, no ads, no sponsored picks. Free in both directions: it costs nothing, and it isn't selling you to anyone.

No cookies, no tracking of you as a person.

What actually leaves your device

Anonymous visit counts. The site uses a privacy-focused counter called Umami (their servers see that a page was visited — not who you are; no cookies, no personal profile). We use it for one thing: knowing whether anyone is using the app at all.

Live data requests. When a gauge or test fetches real market data, your browser talks directly to that public source — CoinGecko, DBnomics (OECD), the CFTC, Hyperliquid, Frankfurter (ECB rates), and public files on GitHub. Those services see a normal web request from your device, the same as visiting any website. We add nothing to it.

Nothing else. There is no server of ours collecting anything — this whole app is static files.

Your own files stay yours

When you load your own CSV into the Signal Test, it is read on your device, in your browser. It is never uploaded anywhere. Close the tab and it's gone.

Why you shouldn't just trust this page

Because you don't have to. The entire app is plain, readable files — on any page, use your browser's view-source and read exactly what it does. The claims above are checkable in the code itself, which is the only privacy promise that actually means anything.

The mirror, as always: this page describes the app as it is today. If we ever add anything that changes the answers above, this page changes the same day — and if it doesn't, the code will contradict us and you should trust the code. Questions or something looks wrong: Xplainsight@duck.com.
