🚧 This room is offline while we rebuild it

Why it's down, plainly: the old flight simulator showed a bare price line — no date labels, no time increments, no volume, no real price scale, just a normalized squiggle. Guessing whether that squiggle goes up, down, or sideways is closer to a coin flip than a reading exercise, and that fails the room's own promise. Per this project's honesty rule, a room that doesn't do what it claims comes down and says so — it doesn't get a softer name and stay live.

Credit where it's due: Nicholas caught this himself, by asking a simple, correct question — "what is there to actually read here?" The answer was: not enough. That's a real bug report, not a matter of taste, and the honest fix is to pull the room, not repaint it.

What the rebuild will actually give you — from real feedback gathered on this project:

  1. Observation first. A real chart — dated axis, price scale, volume — with the question "what do you notice?" before any guess is asked for.
  2. Evidence you request. Volume, trend shape, and (where the data allows) sector or context — revealed progressively, so you're investigating, not just staring.
  3. A one-sentence thesis you write yourself before committing.
  4. A stated confidence — so the grade is calibration (does your sureness match your accuracy), not luck.
  5. The reasoning gets graded, not just the guess — a well-reasoned call that didn't work out is a different, more honest lesson than a right-or-wrong scoreboard.

This is a real rebuild, not a relabel — it needs proper data (dated, volumed, scaled) and new interaction design, so it's taking longer than a quick fix. It will return when it can actually teach what it claims to.
